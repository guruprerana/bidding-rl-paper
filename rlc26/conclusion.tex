\section{Conclusion and Future Directions}
In this work, we consider a special class of multi-objective RL environments in which objectives appear and disappear over time.
We proposed an auction-based mechanism for multi-policy coordination in multi-objective Markov decision processes. 
Multiple selfish policies---each optimizing its own objective---compete with each other to take control of the agent by placing bids.
The mechanism penalizes the policies for placing large bids thereby ensuring that they only bid when necessary.
Also, decomposing the objectives makes the credit assignment problem easier and allows us to design dense reward signals with respect to each objective
We train the policies concurrently using proximal policy optimization (PPO) and evaluate it on a challenging gridworld environment as well as the Atari Assault game.
Our method outperforms baselines under the same compute budget and produces interpretable policies enabling us to determine the current objective that the agent is pursuing.
We also showed that the policies are able to compete with more policies than they were trained with which lets us scale up the number of objectives at deployment time.

While we have only considered multi-objective environments in which the objectives share similar characteristics, we believe that the auction-based mechanism can be adapted to more complex combinations of objectives.
For example, we could train navigation and safety policies that complete reach-avoid objectives.
